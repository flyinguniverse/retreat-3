Mission

Spiritual maturity refers to a deep understanding of oneself, others, and the interconnectedness of all things. It involves transcending the limitations of the ego and cultivating qualities such as compassion, wisdom, patience, and equanimity. Here are a few key aspects that are associated with this form of a spirutual retreat:

Increased Self-Awareness

The Retreat fosters a keen awareness of one's thoughts, emotions, and behaviors. It requires an ongoing process of self-reflection, self-inquiry, and self-discovery.

Inner growth

A spiritually mature person is committed to personal growth and transformation. They actively seek to overcome their limitations, heal emotional wounds, and develop their potential.

Acceptance and non-judgment

Spiritual maturity involves accepting oneself and others without judgment. It recognizes that each person is on their own unique journey and respects the diversity of paths and perspectives.

Transcendence of the EGO

Every Spiritual mature person wants transcend the limited identification with the ego and recognizeg the larger, universal Self. It involves letting go of attachment, ego-driven desires, and the illusion of separation.

Surrender to every form of ...

A spiritually mature person recognizes the limitations of their knowledge and embraces humility. We are open to learning from others, surrendering to higher forces, and accepting the mysteries of life.

Service and contribution

Spiritual maturity leads to a desire to contribute positively to the world. So it is part of the Retreats aftermath. It involves using one's gifts, talents, and resources for the spreading of awakening, the service towards others and the greater good.

Compassion and empathy

The Retreat cultivates a deep sense of compassion and empathy towards all beings. They recognize the interconnectedness of life and extend kindness and understanding to others.
